Feedback

What Did the Pears Say?

Generally I received positive feedback. Yay!

I received some comments about the overlapping data (data density) and some issues with how large the range of the scale is. I had anticipated most of these concerns in the planned interactivity, which my reviewers appreciated. There was a specific suggestion to switch my categorical divergent color scheme to a sequential color scheme, and requests for more categorization about cause of language decline (definitely interesting, but sadly not present in any datasets that I could find).

I did consider changing my color scale to represent the number of speakers, but I was concerned that the huge range of speaker populations would mean that it would be difficult to see distinctions (as it's difficult to see size distinctions now.)

My proposal to add brushing by language family (as opposed to degree of endangerment or size bucket) was well-received, and I'm very pleased I was able to implement it. I think it really does add more information to the map.

My final visualization still has some issues with overlapping data bubbles -- either the largest bubbles (languages with millions of speakers) are massive and cover up whole continents, or the entire scale is shifted smaller, introducing some lie factor as there's less distinction between smaller bubbles. I hope that the zoom and filtering capabilities will help alleviate this issue.

In the original feedback session, Prof. Engle brought up the idea of presenting actual text samples from each language, potentially as a wordcloud. There were a number of other directions I could have gone with for this dataset -- another obvious idea is a hierarchical visualization to show the relationships between families, languages, and dialects. Those would be interesting avenues to pursue in the future.

I have done my best to replicate the feedback I received -- I should have taken better notes during the live synchronous feedback session!
